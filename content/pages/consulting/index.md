I help organizations in the education sector use data and strategy to achieve meaningful, equitable outcomes for students and communities.

## What I Do

### Educational Policy Analysis

Education policy looks different on paper than it does in practice. I examine how policies at the local, state, and federal levels actually play out in schools and districts: where implementation breaks down, where unintended consequences emerge, and what leaders can do about it. This work helps organizations respond to policy change strategically rather than reactively.

### Program Evaluation

Good intentions aren't enough. I design and conduct studies to determine whether educational programs are actually achieving their goals, not just whether stakeholders like them. That means choosing the right methods for the question, building in rigor from the start, and producing findings that are honest even when the results are complicated.

### Data Analysis

Most organizations are sitting on more useful data, expertise, and information than they realize. I work with educational data to surface meaningful patterns and actionable insights. The goal is always to connect the analysis to decisions your organization actually needs to make, while leveraging your existing organizational strenghts.

### Strategic Planning

Clarity about direction is harder to achieve than it sounds. I help organizations figure out where they want to go and build realistic paths to get there, grounded in an honest assessment of their current situation. That means engaging the people who know the organization best, looking clearly at what the data shows, and making deliberate choices about priorities.

### Professional Development

Most professional development doesn't change what happens in classrooms. I design learning experiences for educators that are built to do exactly that: connecting new knowledge and skills to the specific challenges teachers and leaders face in their contexts. Sessions are interactive and practical by design, not just informational.

### Grant Support

A strong grant proposal is more than compelling writing. I help develop proposals that are grounded in evidence, realistic about implementation, and built around rigorous evaluation plans from the start. That approach strengthens both the proposal and the work itself.

## How I Work

I combine solid research methods with practical judgment about what organizations can actually use. A few principles guide every engagement:

### Collaborative Partnership

I believe the best consulting work happens through genuine partnership, not through me parachuting in with all the answers. When we work together, I start by listening to understand your unique context, challenges, and aspirations. You bring deep expertise about your organization that I could never gain from an outside perspective alone. My role is to complement that internal knowledge with external perspective, research skills, and analytical tools. We co-create solutions that make sense for your specific situation, rather than me imposing a preconceived framework that might not fit.

### Evidence-Based Approach

My recommendations are grounded in evidence, not assumptions or generic frameworks that get applied to every client regardless of their situation. I invest time upfront to gather relevant data, whether that's quantitative metrics, qualitative feedback from stakeholders, document reviews, or observations of practice. I analyze this information carefully to identify patterns, root causes, and opportunities that might not be immediately apparent. This evidence-based approach means our work together is focused on what's actually happening in your organization, not on what theory says should be happening. It also provides a solid foundation for measuring progress and impact over time.

### Practical Implementation Focus

While it's tempting to pursue elegant theoretical solutions, I focus on what's actually implementable given your organization's resources, capacity, and constraints. I ask practical questions: What can you realistically accomplish with your current staff and budget? What timing makes sense given your other priorities? What barriers might get in the way, and how can we address them proactively? I help you distinguish between what's nice to have in an ideal world and what will make a meaningful difference in your current reality.

### Capacity Building

My ultimate goal isn't to create ongoing dependency on my consulting services, but to leave your organization stronger and more capable than when we started. I approach every engagement with an eye toward knowledge transfer and skill development. Whether it's documenting processes so your team can continue the work independently, providing training on new methodologies, or coaching leaders through challenging transitions, I focus on building internal capacity. I want you to be able to tackle similar challenges in the future without needing to bring in external help every time. This approach ensures that the value of our work together extends well beyond the formal engagement period.

## Types of Engagements

I shape my work around what you actually need, not predetermined packages.

### Focused Projects

Some questions have a clear scope and a defined finish line. Focused projects are built around a specific deliverable or question—an evaluation, an analysis, a strategic plan—and typically run one to three months. This structure works well when you have a pressing need and want concentrated effort on a well-defined problem.

### Ongoing Advisory Support

Some work unfolds over time and benefits from a sustained outside perspective. Ongoing advisory support provides regular engagement with leadership teams or initiative teams: thinking through emerging challenges, reviewing decisions, and helping the organization stay oriented toward its goals as circumstances change.

### Workshops and Training

Sometimes the most valuable thing is building your team's capacity directly. I design and facilitate interactive workshops tailored to your team's specific context and goals. Sessions can focus on anything from data use and program evaluation to strategic thinking and planning processes.

### Speaking

I present at conferences, convenings, and internal meetings on topics related to education data, policy, evaluation, and strategy. If you're looking for someone to anchor a session or contribute a practitioner perspective to a broader event, I'm glad to discuss what might fit.

## Who I Work With

My clients are typically organizations that are serious about improvement. You'll find me working with district leaders on policy changes, and with various education and nonprofit organizations on evaluation and evidence-building efforts. I'm most useful when there's a real question to answer and a genuine commitment to acting on what you discover.
